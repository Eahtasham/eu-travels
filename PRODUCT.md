# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective leisure and luxury travelers browsing for curated trips — often in a
dreaming/inspiration mindset rather than a transactional one. They arrive on
desktop or mobile, want to feel the possibility of a journey, and need enough
trust signals (destinations, services, social proof) to take the first step:
planning a trip.

## Product Purpose

EU Travels is a premium travel-agency landing page that turns wanderlust
into booked journeys. It showcases destinations, services, and social proof
through a scroll-driven "flight above the clouds" narrative, converting browsers
into "plan your journey" leads. Success = a visitor who feels calm confidence in
the brand and reaches for the primary CTA.

## Brand Personality

Serene, elevated, premium. Calm confidence and first-class ease — the tranquility
of being above the clouds. Voice: warm, refined, aspirational but grounded. Never
loud, never discount-bargain, never busy.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI SaaS landing pages (centered stack, pill eyebrow on every section,
  identical icon-card grids).
- Cluttered discount-travel aggregators (Expedia / Booking.com density and noise).
- Loud neon "adventure-bro" aesthetics.
- Cream/beige editorial-magazine template (display-serif-italic + broadsheet grid)
  applied by reflex.

## Design Principles

- **Let the sky breathe.** The scroll imagery and negative space carry the mood;
  don't crowd it. One dominant idea per fold.
- **Legible over pretty-but-unreadable.** Text sits on a shifting photographic
  sky; guarantee contrast with owned scrims, not luck.
- **Quiet confidence.** Restraint and precision over ornament. Elegance from
  spacing, type, and pacing.
- **Motion as calm choreography.** Gentle, intentional reveals with slow ease-out.
  Never bouncy, elastic, or busy.
- **Robust across the journey.** The scroll-driven frames change constantly; the
  UI must stay legible and stable at every frame and every breakpoint.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast for all text (verified against the actual sky frames, not a flat
color). Visible `:focus-visible` indicators on every control. `prefers-reduced-motion`
honored for entrance motion, the scroll cue, and the loader. Touch targets ≥44px.
Semantic headings, labeled icon-only controls, decorative canvas hidden from AT.
